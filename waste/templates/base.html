Waste Management


Waste management involves the collection, transportation, and disposal of garbage, sewage, and other waste products. Effective waste management is crucial for maintaining cleanliness, preventing environmental pollution, and promoting sustainable development.


Proper waste management practices include recycling, composting, and safe disposal methods to minimize the impact on our environment. By managing waste responsibly, we can contribute to a healthier and cleaner community for future generations.



Landfill

A landfill is a managed site where waste is buried under the ground. It's a method used for garbage disposal that aims to control environmental impact.

Incineration

Incineration is a waste treatment process that involves the combustion of organic substances contained in waste materials. It is used to convert waste into ash, flue gas, and heat.

Biogas

Biogas is a renewable energy produced from the anaerobic digestion of organic waste, mainly consisting of methane and carbon dioxide.

Pyrolysis

Pyrolysis and gasification are innovative technologies transforming waste into valuable resources through controlled thermal decomposition.

Composting

Composting enriches soil health by recycling organic waste into nutrient-rich fertilizer, promoting sustainable agriculture.
